# Code of Conduct

This repository is for proposing, debating, and refining a piece of public
policy. To make that work, contributors agree to a few simple rules.

## Engage with the policy, not the person

Attack arguments, not people. Disagree with what is being said, not who is
saying it. Personal attacks, slurs, harassment, and ad hominem will be
removed and the contributor blocked.

## Steel-man before you attack

Before you argue against a position, demonstrate that you understand its
strongest form. This applies to suggestions, dissent files, and pull
request reviews equally.

## Cite when you can

If you're proposing a policy change based on an external fact (a budget
number, a Statistics Canada figure, a court ruling, another city's bylaw),
cite the source. Unverifiable claims are harder to take seriously.

## Stay on topic

This repo is about one bylaw. Suggestions about other policies, candidates,
or city issues belong elsewhere.

## Enforcement

The repo maintainer may close, hide, or remove any content that violates
this code, and may block contributors who do so repeatedly.

## Reporting

If you see something that violates this code, comment on the issue or PR,
or email the contact listed at [ward9.online](https://ward9.online).
