### Q1 — New s. 4.3(a)(vi) Fiscal Year Definition

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

The language precisely tracks *Municipal Act*, s. 285 ("the fiscal year of every municipality is a calendar year") and names the January 1–December 31 period explicitly. The phrase "for the purposes of this section" correctly scopes the definition to s. 4.3 without disturbing any other Policy provision that may use "fiscal year" in a different context. No further fix required.

***

### Q2 — New s. 4.3(e)(iv) and (v) — Timing and Non-Compliance

**Verdict: CONFIRMED for both Defect 2 and the Section 8 recommendation, with one minor precision fix suggested**

**(iv) — Interim calculation timing:** The provision correctly identifies the gap scenario (rating by-law not adopted before first payment date) and prescribes the default (prior-year decrement) and reconciliation trigger (first payment after adoption). This is operationally workable and closes Defect 2.

**(v) — Non-compliance / retroactive reconciliation:** The provision correctly imposes a duty of prompt catch-up, retroactive application, and next-payment reconciliation for any of the three sub-duties (calculate, record, publish). This addresses the Section 8 mandamus concern by making the duty express and self-executing — it eliminates the factual ambiguity about whether a late calculation is a nullity or merely delayed.

**One minor precision fix:** The phrase "deadlines normally observed" in (v) is informal and potentially unenforceable because no specific deadlines are "normally observed" in the operative text — the by-law itself does not set a deadline in (i), (ii), or (iii), only an annual obligation. Replace with: *"where any calculation under subsection (i), maintenance of records under subsection (ii), or publication under subsection (iii) has not been completed before the date on which the relevant annual rating by-law is adopted."* This ties the lateness trigger to a defined event (adoption of the rating by-law) rather than the vague standard of normalcy, and is consistent with the architecture of (iv).

**Interaction between (iv) and (v):** Addressed below under Q5(a).

### Q3 — s. 4.3(c)(i) Compounding Clarification Addition

**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

The added phrase — *"and without including any reduction attributable to the accumulated decrement from prior or current fiscal years"* — sitting alongside the existing *"all without reference to the accumulated decrement"* — forecloses the compounding-base argument identified in the v13 audit. The two clauses are additive and complementary: the first is a general exclusion instruction; the second is a specific mathematical exclusion. Together they make it unambiguous that the s. 4.2 input trajectory is entirely pre-decrement in every year, including years where the prior year's pay was itself reduced by the decrement. No residual ambiguity remains on this point.

Tension/redundancy analysis: see Q5(b).

### Q4 — Appendix A Enacting Language

**Verdict: IMPRECISE — two improvements required**

**Primary clause (v12-was-enacted scenario):**

The language is structurally correct — repeal of the prior section followed by substitution of the new text is the standard Ontario municipal by-law amendment form. However, the placeholder "[v12 carrying by-law reference, if applicable]" should be resolved before introduction. If v12 was never formally enacted as a by-law (i.e., it remained a campaign proposal), the alternative clause applies and the primary clause should be deleted in its entirety from the clean draft. Leaving the placeholder unresolved in the introduced version creates an ambiguity about which prior provision is being repealed.

**Recommended improvement to the primary clause:** Specify not just the section being repealed but also the Policy version date:

> *"Section 4.3 of City of London Policy CPOL.-70(b)-220 (Remuneration for Elected Officials and Appointed Citizen Members Policy), as last amended by [by-law number and date], is hereby repealed and the following substituted therefor as the new section 4.3 of that Policy:"*

**Alternative clause (v12-never-enacted scenario):** The alternative language is sound except for one gap — the phrase "any prior provision of section 4 of the Policy" is broad enough to potentially be read as overriding even s. 4.2 in cases of alleged conflict. Given that s. 4.3(h) already establishes internal priority limited to "interpretation conflicts within section 4 … relating to annual adjustment, base compensation, and levy-linkage," the alternative clause's conflict-resolution language should mirror that same scope:

> *"To the extent that any prior provision of section 4 of the Policy relating to annual adjustment, base compensation, or levy-linkage is inconsistent with the new section 4.3, the new section 4.3 prevails."*

**Coming-into-force clause:** The current *"This by-law comes into force on the date of its passage"* is standard and legally effective. No change required. (The Section 18.2 CIF recital issue remains a downstream choice, as the campaign has decided.)

### Q5 — New Exposure / Interaction Effects

#### Q5(a) — Interaction Between New (iv) and New (v)

**Verdict: IMPRECISE — overlap is manageable but one clarification helps**

(iv) governs the specific scenario where the *rating by-law has not been adopted before the first payment date*. (v) governs *any lateness in the three Treasurer duties generally*. The two provisions partially overlap: if the rating by-law is adopted late, (iv) supplies the interim mechanism, but (v) could also be read to apply to the same lateness as a "failure to calculate" under (i).

The practical risk is that (iv) and (v) could be read as prescribing different reconciliation timing for the same event — (iv) reconciles "in the first remuneration payment *following adoption*," while (v) reconciles "in the next remuneration payment *following the calculation*." In the normal case these will be the same payment, but if the Treasurer calculates after adoption but before the next payment, they may differ by one payment cycle.

**Suggested fix:** Add a sequencing priority sentence at the start of (v): *"Without limiting subsection (iv), where…"* This makes (iv) the lex specialis for the rating-by-law-lateness scenario and (v) the lex generalis for all other lateness, eliminating any ambiguity about which timing rule governs the overlap scenario.

#### Q5(b) — Tension / Redundancy Between the Two Phrases in s. 4.3(c)(i)

**Verdict: CONFIRMED — no harmful tension; characterise as belt-and-suspenders**

*"all without reference to the accumulated decrement"* is a general methodological instruction (do not look at the decrement ledger at all when computing the s. 4.2 figure). *"without including any reduction attributable to the accumulated decrement from prior or current fiscal years"* is a specific mathematical instruction (the base you use must not have been reduced by a prior decrement). They are logically consistent and sequentially coherent — the general instruction covers the process, the specific instruction covers the output. A court interpreting the provision would read them as complementary, not contradictory. The specific phrase does useful independent work by foreclosing the compounding-base argument even in a hypothetical scenario where a future drafter or administrator might argue the general phrase only governs the current-year calculation.

No drafting change required.

#### Q5(c) — New Fiscal-Year Definition Conflicting With Implicit References Elsewhere in s. 4.3

**Verdict: CONFIRMED — no conflict introduced**

The new s. 4.3(a)(vi) defines "fiscal year" *"for the purposes of this section"* — it is explicitly scoped. Every use of "fiscal year" in s. 4.3(b), (c), (d), (e), (f), and (g) is governed by this definition. Because the City of London's actual fiscal year *is* January 1–December 31 (consistent with *Municipal Act* s. 285), the definition is not creating a new meaning — it is codifying the existing statutory reality. There is no provision within s. 4.3 that uses "fiscal year" in a way that would be disrupted by this definition.

One theoretical edge case: if the Province ever amended *Municipal Act* s. 285 to permit non-calendar fiscal years, the s. 4.3(a)(vi) definition would be internally consistent but might diverge from a future City practice. This is a vanishingly remote scenario and no fix is warranted. The *"within the meaning of section 285"* cross-reference would continue to anchor the definition to whatever s. 285 provides at any given time.

### Q7 — Other Defects or Improvement Opportunities

Two items surfaced:

**7.1 — s. 4.3(e)(v) "as soon as practicable" standard**

This phrase is a well-established legal standard in Ontario administrative law, but it creates no enforcement deadline. A Treasurer who waits three months to perform a late calculation is technically compliant. If the campaign wants a backstop, consider adding *"and in any event no later than 60 days after the end of the relevant fiscal year"* after "as soon as practicable" in (v). This is a design choice, not a legal requirement.

**7.2 — No Conflict Between v13.1 and the New s. 4.3(e)(iv)/(v) Reconciliation Mechanism and OMERS**

The v13 audit (Section 18.3) flagged the OMERS pension interaction. The new interim-calculation mechanism in (iv) creates a scenario where the remuneration paid in Q1 of a fiscal year differs from the reconciled final amount. OMERS contributions are typically based on amounts actually paid per payroll period. A mid-year reconciliation payment (positive or negative) will need to be treated correctly under the OMERS contribution rules — specifically, whether a reconciliation deduction in one pay period is treated as a reduction in pensionable earnings for that period or as a correction to the prior period. This is an OMERS administration question, not a drafting defect in v13.1, but it should be flagged to the City's OMERS administrator concurrently with the operational briefing recommended in the v13 next steps.
