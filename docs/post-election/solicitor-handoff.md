This memo identifies the items that must be closed before the Pay Accountable to City Taxpayers (PACT) amending by-law can be introduced at Council. The proposal is fully drafted at `Campaign/research/pact-refactor-proposal-2026-05-21-v12.md`. ## Revision 2 — what changed since the first cut

Perplexity Pro Deep Research pass on 2026-05-22 (209 sources, full report at `PACT Bylaw Refactor Proposal v12 — Comprehensive Audit Report.md`) confirmed v12 is structurally sound and produced four findings that strengthen the package handed to the City Solicitor:

1. **Municipal Conflict of Interest Act s. 4(i) exemption confirmed.** All members of Council (Mayor and 14 Councillors) may vote on PACT without disclosure or disqualification. The pecuniary interest each member has in their own future remuneration is explicitly exempted from MCIA ss. 5, 5.2, and 5.3 by s. 4(i) ("remuneration… to which the member may be entitled by reason of being a member"). This unblocks an unstated assumption.
2. **Standard of review is now *Auer v. Auer* (2024 SCC) reasonableness with presumption of validity.** The November 2024 SCC decision in *Auer v. Auer*, 2024 SCC 36 (affirmed in *TransAlta Generation Partnership v. Alberta*) settled the standard of review for subordinate legislation including municipal by-laws at reasonableness, with a presumption of validity. A challenger must show the by-law "does not fall within a reasonable interpretation of the delegate's statutory authority." PACT's anchoring in Municipal Act s. 5(3), s. 8(1), s. 8(2), and s. 11(2) paragraphs 1 and 3 satisfies this test by a comfortable margin.
3. **Plain reading of s. 4.2(b) suppresses positive adjustments only.** The phrase "no increase shall be applied" in a wage-freeze pause clause is, on Ontario plain-meaning interpretation, restricted to upward adjustments. The "notwithstanding subsections 4.2(a) and 4.2(b)" language in s. 4.3(b) is therefore belt-and-suspenders, not load-bearing. This reduces (though does not eliminate) the legal-risk surface for Item 1 below.
4. **Worked example ready for SPPC presentation.** If PACT passes in late 2026 (the new Council's first term), the first adjustment applies on January 1, 2027, calculated against the 2026 levy increase of approximately 3.4%. **First PACT-driven reduction: approximately 3.4 percentage points off the section 4.2 annual adjustment.** Concrete, immediate, publicly communicable.

## Item 1 — Counsel opinion on the "notwithstanding 4.2(a) and 4.2(b)" override

**Affects:** v12 section 4.3(b) and 4.3(c) in the proposed amending by-law (Part 6 of the proposal).

**The question (four sub-questions):**

a) Can an amending by-law in the CPOL.-70 series legally override earlier subsections within the same by-law (CPOL.-70(b)-220 s. 4.2(a) and s. 4.2(b)) by means of express "notwithstanding" drafting?

b) Is the resulting downward adjustment (where the section 4.2 annual indexing percentage minus the levy increase percentage produces a negative number) lawful under the Municipal Act, 2001 and any related provincial regulations?

c) How should s. 4.2(b) ("in those years where non-union staff wages are frozen, no increase shall be applied") be interpreted? Two readings are possible:

   - Reading A (narrow): "no increase shall be applied" suppresses positive adjustments only. Under this reading, PACT's negative adjustment can still apply in a non-union wage-freeze year.
   - Reading B (broad): the clause is shorthand for "no adjustment shall be applied" in either direction. Under this reading, the s. 4.2 annual adjustment is paused in a freeze year and PACT's negative adjustment is also suppressed.

   Which reading governs?

d) If Reading B governs, is overriding s. 4.2(b) in a non-union wage-freeze year a lawful and defensible drafting choice via the proposed "notwithstanding subsections 4.2(a) and 4.2(b)" override?

**Proposed answer / framing (to confirm or correct):**

The "notwithstanding" technique is the standard municipal drafting mechanism for resolving internal conflicts within a single by-law instrument and is regularly used in Ontario CPOL-series amendments. The amendment does not claw back previously-paid remuneration. It modifies the FUTURE annual adjustment component for the next January 1 application. Under Municipal Act s. 8(1) ("powers shall be interpreted broadly") and s. 8(2) (ambiguity resolved in favour of inclusion), a remuneration adjustment formula that can produce a downward outcome is within municipal authority. Reading A on s. 4.2(b) is the natural Ontario plain-meaning reading: "no increase shall be applied" suppresses positive adjustments only ("increase" is not synonymous with "adjustment"; a negative adjustment is a decrease, not an increase). Under Reading A, s. 4.2(b) presents zero conflict with PACT's downward adjustment and the "notwithstanding 4.2(b)" language is belt-and-suspenders. Reading B is the broader plausible interpretation that the express "notwithstanding" override is designed to resolve in either case.

**Authority-base reinforcement (Deep Research finding 2):** The November 2024 Supreme Court of Canada decision in *Auer v. Auer*, 2024 SCC 36 (and the companion *TransAlta Generation Partnership v. Alberta* decision) settled the standard of review for subordinate legislation including municipal by-laws at reasonableness, with a presumption of validity. A challenger must show the by-law "does not fall within a reasonable interpretation of the delegate's statutory authority." PACT, anchored in s. 5(3), s. 8(1), s. 8(2), and s. 11(2) paragraphs 1 (governance structure) and 3 (financial management), with WHEREAS clauses tying the mechanism to fiscal accountability and alignment between remuneration and the municipality's fiscal capacity decisions, satisfies this test. No reported Ontario decision strikes down an intra-instrument "notwithstanding" clause of this type. No reported Ontario decision strikes down a remuneration by-law for producing a downward formula adjustment — the absence likely reflects the novelty of the mechanism in Ontario practice rather than adverse authority.

## Item 2 — Treasurer mapping of "total municipal tax levy" to London's actual annual rating by-law

**Affects:** v12 section 4.3(a)(ii) and 4.3(a)(iii) (the definitions in the amending by-law).

**The question (four sub-questions):**

a) Does London's annual rating by-law (passed each year under Municipal Act s. 312(2)) report the total amount levied for municipal purposes as a single discrete number in a specific section or schedule? Where in the by-law is that number located (section, schedule, line item)?

b) Is the education portion (set by the Province) reported as a separately identifiable line item that can be cleanly excluded from the "municipal purposes" total?

c) Is the MPAC-driven assessment-value-change component separately identifiable in the rating by-law, or is it embedded in the calculation of the rates without a discrete extractable figure?

d) If any of (a) through (c) cannot be cleanly mapped to London's rating by-law as currently presented, what specific London-published category names, schedule headings, or table line numbers should the PACT amendment text reference instead?

**Proposed answer / framing (to confirm or correct):**

The PACT amendment text v12 defines "total municipal tax levy" as the figure identified in the annual rating by-law as the amount to be levied for municipal purposes, excluding education and MPAC components "to the extent each is separately identified" in the by-law. If London's rating by-law presents the figures cleanly, this drafting is sufficient. If not, the City Treasurer should suggest a more specific cross-reference (e.g., "the figure shown on Schedule X, Section Y of the annual rating by-law, identified as 'Municipal Purposes Total'").

**Documents that would close this item:**

- The 2024 annual rating by-law passed by Council (PDF)
- The 2025 annual rating by-law passed by Council (PDF)
- A short Treasurer note identifying the discrete line items corresponding to (a), (b), and (c) above for both years

## Item 3 — Counsel confirmation that the Municipal Act authorizes formula-driven downward remuneration adjustments

**Affects:** the overall structural defensibility of v12, the WHEREAS clauses, and section 4.3(c) (which explicitly permits a negative annual adjustment).

**The question:**

Does the Municipal Act, 2001 authorize a council remuneration by-law that produces formula-driven downward adjustments based on Council's other fiscal decisions (specifically the annual property tax levy adopted under s. 312(2))? Are there any provisions in the Act, related regulations, or Ontario case law that constrain remuneration by-laws to upward-only or floor-protected adjustments?

**Proposed answer / framing (to confirm or correct):**

The Municipal Act, 2001, in sections 5(3), 8(1), 8(2), and 11(2) paragraphs 1 (governance structure) and 3 (financial management), grants broad permissive authority to council to enact by-laws on these matters. Section 8(1) explicitly directs that "powers shall be interpreted broadly so as to confer broad authority on the municipality to enable the municipality to govern its affairs as it considers appropriate." Section 8(2) directs that "in the event of ambiguity in whether or not a municipality has the authority... to pass a by-law or to take any other action, the ambiguity shall be resolved so as to include, rather than exclude, powers the municipality had on the day before this Act came into force." There is no express provision in the Act that requires remuneration adjustments to be upward-only or floor-protected. The existing CPOL.-70(b)-220 s. 4.2(a) zero floor is itself a Council Policy choice, not a statutory floor; an amending by-law can therefore modify or override it. The PACT amendment does not claw back already-paid remuneration. It modifies the formula by which the FUTURE annual adjustment component is calculated. This is within the broad authority granted by ss. 5, 8, and 11.

- Verbatim Municipal Act s. 5, 8, 9, 11 (Part 1 of the proposal)

## Item 4 — Confirmation that By-law CPOL.-70(b)-220 covers the Mayor (belt-and-suspenders)

**Affects:** v12 section 4.3(a)(iv) definition of "remuneration," and the policy intent that PACT applies to all elected officials including the Mayor.

**The question:**

CPOL.-70(b)-220 uses the term "elected officials" throughout sections 1.1, 3.1, and 4.2 without an internal definition (section 2.1 says "Not applicable"). Does "elected officials" in this Policy include the Mayor, such that the annual indexing under s. 4.2 applies to the Mayor's remuneration as well as Councillors'?

**Proposed answer / framing (to confirm or correct):**

In standard Ontario municipal usage, "elected officials" of a municipality means the head of council (Mayor) and the other members of council (Councillors). The 2026 Council Guide states: "compensation for 2026 is indexed in accordance with the existing Remuneration for Elected Officials and Appointed Citizen Members Policy and annualized," referring to both Mayor ($168,908) and Councillor ($94,222) base figures. Resolution 2025-C06 clause (e) "maintains" the Mayor's current compensation as a separate one-time decision; it does not exempt the Mayor from section 4.2's annual indexing. The PACT amending by-law therefore applies to all elected officials covered by section 4.2, including the Mayor.

**Note for counsel:** Confirmation is requested as a belt-and-suspenders measure. The amendment text would not change based on the answer; the Mayor is automatically captured because the amendment modifies section 4.2, which already applies to all elected officials.

## Item 5 — Confirmation of MCIA s. 4(i) exemption for the introducing and adopting votes

**Affects:** The Council vote(s) to introduce, debate, and pass the PACT amending by-law.

**The question:**

Does the Municipal Conflict of Interest Act, R.S.O. 1990, c. M.50, s. 4(i) exemption fully apply to each member of Council with respect to votes on the PACT amending by-law, such that no member is required to declare a pecuniary interest, recuse, or refrain from voting?

**Proposed answer / framing (to confirm or correct):**

Section 4(i) of the MCIA reads: *"Sections 5, 5.2 and 5.3 do not apply to a pecuniary interest in a matter that a member may have… (i) in respect of an allowance for attendance at meetings, or any other allowance, honorarium, remuneration, salary or benefit to which the member may be entitled by reason of being a member."* Each member's pecuniary interest in PACT arises because PACT modifies the annual adjustment formula applicable to their own remuneration by reason of being a member of Council. The exemption is directly on point. All fifteen members of Council (Mayor plus 14 Councillors) may vote on introduction, debate, and passage of the amending by-law without MCIA exposure. The same exemption applies to the Mayor's use of strong-mayor budget proposal powers (s. 5.3 of the MCIA) with respect to matters touching councillor remuneration.

**Note for counsel:** Confirmation is requested as a defensive measure. If the Solicitor concurs, an SPPC referral motion can be moved with all members present and voting. If the Solicitor identifies a narrower reading or a tertiary consideration (e.g., a closely-held individual benefit beyond standard remuneration), it should be flagged.

## Worked example for SPPC introduction (Finding D from the Deep Research)

If the PACT amending by-law is passed at SPPC in the late-2026 / early-2027 cycle, the first January 1 occurring after the by-law comes into force will be January 1, 2027. The section 4.2 adjustment for that date uses the 2026 levy figure compared to the 2025 levy figure.

| Year | Approved tax-levy increase | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 8.7% | Approved March 2024 in the original Multi-Year Budget |
| 2025 | 7.3% | Approved December 2024 Annual Update; reduced from the originally-budgeted 8.7% |
| 2026 | ~3.4% | Approved December 1, 2025 Annual Update; reduced from 3.6% at Budget Committee |
| 2027 | 4.7% (proposed) | Mayor's proposed 2027 update; not yet approved as of this memo |

**First PACT-driven reduction:** under the formula in section 4.3(b), the section 4.2 adjustment that would otherwise apply on January 1, 2027 would be reduced by approximately 3.4 percentage points (the 2026 levy increase). For the section 4.2 adjustment to be exactly zero in 2027, the section 4.2 raw adjustment computed from the most recent census variation in median full-time employment income would have to equal exactly 3.4%. For the section 4.2 adjustment to be negative, the raw adjustment would need to be less than 3.4%.

This is a concrete, immediate, publicly communicable consequence of the by-law's passage and is suitable for the SPPC introduction presentation.

## Campaign-side response prep (Finding B from the Deep Research)

One operational nuance for campaign messaging: under PACT, the "total municipal tax levy" includes revenue from MPAC-driven assessment growth, not just from Council's rate decisions. In a year where Council holds the rate flat but assessment grows by 2%, the levy increases by approximately 2% and PACT produces a corresponding 2-percentage-point reduction.

**One-line response to the "PACT punishes councillors for provincial assessment decisions" critique:** *"The total municipal tax levy is what property owners pay to the City for municipal purposes. If Council wants to offset assessment growth, they can cut the rate — that's itself a Council decision."*
