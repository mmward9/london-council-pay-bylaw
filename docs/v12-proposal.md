## Executive summary

The original PACT Bylaw was drafted as a standalone bylaw that reinvented the entire municipal compensation-setting apparatus. Verification against primary sources shows that approach is unnecessarily large.

The City of London already has a complete remuneration framework. By-law CPOL.-70(b)-220 sets the annual indexing formula in section 4.2 and (per standard Ontario municipal-law usage of "elected officials") applies to both Mayor and Councillors. The November 4, 2025 Council resolution 2025-C06 adopted the 2025 Council Resourcing Review Task Force recommendation to repeg the councillor compensation base to the 70th percentile of full-time employment income for Londoners per the most recent census, while maintaining the section 4.2 annual indexing. The piece the City's framework lacks is the only piece PACT actually needs to add: a link between the property tax levy council adopts and the pay council pays itself.

The refactored PACT is a single amending by-law in the CPOL.-70 series that adds a new section 4.3 to CPOL.-70(b)-220. The City's existing pay-setting apparatus continues to run untouched in years when the levy is flat or declining. In years when council raises the total municipal tax levy adopted under the annual rating by-law, the otherwise-payable annual adjustment is reduced by the same number of percentage points as the levy increase. There is no floor on the resulting pay change. The reduction applies to all elected official remuneration covered by the Policy, including the 8%-of-base stipends for the Deputy Mayor and Budget Chair contemplated under resolution 2025-C06 clause (h), as and when those stipends are formalized in the Policy.

Municipal Act section 283 (or its current successor provision) is the statutory authority for council remuneration by-laws in Ontario. The PACT amending by-law uses the same authority and procedure used to enact the existing by-law and its two prior amendments. Strong-mayor veto exposure under O. Reg. 530/22 is the same as for any by-law amendment in this area; the refactor does not reduce that exposure.

This document covers (1) the statutory authority, (2) the existing City framework, (3) the November 4, 2025 Council resolution, (4) verified figures, (5) the refactor design and the interaction with clause (c) automatic administration, (6) the exact amending by-law text, (7) the implementation path, and (8) items still requiring verification.

## Part 1: Statutory authority (verbatim)

### Council's authority to pay remuneration to its members

In Ontario, after the Municipal Statute Law Amendment Act, 2006 (Bill 130), council's authority to pay remuneration to its members rests on the BROAD PERMISSIVE GRANT in sections 5, 8, and 11 of the Municipal Act, 2001, rather than on any single specific-grant section. The relevant provisions, verbatim:

> **5 (3)** A municipal power, including a municipality's capacity, rights, powers and privileges under section 9, shall be exercised by by-law unless the municipality is specifically authorized to do otherwise. 2001, c. 25, s. 5 (3); 2006, c. 32, Sched. A, s. 5.
>
> **8 (1)** The powers of a municipality under this or any other Act shall be interpreted broadly so as to confer broad authority on the municipality to enable the municipality to govern its affairs as it considers appropriate and to enhance the municipality's ability to respond to municipal issues. 2006, c. 32, Sched. A, s. 8.
>
> **8 (2)** In the event of ambiguity in whether or not a municipality has the authority under this or any other Act to pass a by-law or to take any other action, the ambiguity shall be resolved so as to include, rather than exclude, powers the municipality had on the day before this Act came into force. 2006, c. 32, Sched. A, s. 8.
>
> **11 (2)** A lower-tier municipality and an upper-tier municipality may pass by-laws, subject to the rules set out in subsection (4), respecting the following matters:
>
> 1. Governance structure of the municipality and its local boards.
> 2. Accountability and transparency of the municipality and its operations and of its local boards and their operations.
> 3. Financial management of the municipality and its local boards.
> [...remaining items continue]
>
> 2006, c. 32, Sched. A, s. 8; 2017, c. 10, Sched. 1, s. 2.

Source: [Ontario Municipal Act, 2001, retrieved as Word document from ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/laws/docs/01m25_e.doc), section 5, 8, and 11.

A council remuneration by-law (paragraph 1, "Governance structure," and paragraph 3, "Financial management" of section 11(2)) is therefore enacted under the broad authority in s. 5(3), s. 8(1), and s. 11(2). London's existing By-law CPOL.-70(b)-220 is enacted under this same authority, as are its two prior amending by-laws (CPOL.-70(a)-408 and CPOL.-70(b)-220 itself).

### Section 283 (verbatim) — what it actually says

Section 283 is sometimes cited by other Ontario municipalities (e.g., Kenora, Asphodel-Norwood) as the authority for council pay. **This is incorrect.** Section 283 governs local board remuneration, expense limitations for council and local board members, and transitional rules for resolutions passed under the OLD Municipal Act's section 255(2). The current verbatim text:

> **283 (1)** A municipality may pay any part of the remuneration and expenses of the members of any local board of the municipality and of the officers and employees of the local board. 2001, c. 25, s. 283 (1).
>
> **(2)** Despite any Act, a municipality may only pay the expenses of the members of its council or of a local board of the municipality and of the officers and employees of the municipality or local board if the expenses are of those persons in their capacity as members, officers or employees and if,
>
> (a) the expenses are actually incurred; or
>
> (b) the expenses are, in lieu of the expenses actually incurred, a reasonable estimate, in the opinion of the council or local board, of the actual expenses that would be incurred. 2001, c. 25, s. 283 (2).
>
> **(3)** A local board of a municipality may pay remuneration to and the expenses incurred by its members, officers and employees to the extent that the municipality is able to do so under this Act. 2001, c. 25, s. 283 (3).
>
> **(4)** No part of the remuneration of a member of a council or local board paid under this section is deemed to be for expenses incidental to his or her duties as a member and a municipality or local board shall not provide that any part of the remuneration is for such deemed expenses. 2001, c. 25, s. 283 (4).
>
> **(5)** Despite subsection (4), if a resolution of a municipality under subsection 255 (2) or (3) of the old Act is not revoked before January 1, 2003, the resolution shall be deemed to be a by-law of the municipality and one-third of the remuneration paid to the elected members of the council and its local boards is deemed as expenses incident to the discharge of their duties as members of the council or local board. 2001, c. 25, s. 283 (5); 2002, c. 17, Sched. A, s. 46 (1).
>
> **(6)** A council may repeal a by-law under subsection (5) and the repealing by-law shall be effective on January 1 of the year after the year in which it is passed. 2001, c. 25, s. 283 (6).
>
> **(7)** On or after December 1, 2003, a council shall review a by-law under subsection (5) at a public meeting at least once during the four-year period corresponding to the term of office of its members after a regular election. 2001, c. 25, s. 283 (7); 2002, c. 17, Sched. A, s. 46 (2); 2006, c. 9, Sched. H, s. 5 (4).
>
> **(8)-(10)** [Transitional provisions specific to Mississauga and Markham.]

Source: [Ontario Municipal Act, 2001, retrieved as Word document](https://www.ontario.ca/laws/docs/01m25_e.doc), section 283.

Section 283 has interpretive RELEVANCE to PACT (its subsection (4) confirms remuneration is not deemed expenses, and subsection (2) constrains expense payments), but it is NOT the source of authority. The PACT amending by-law's WHEREAS clauses cite ss. 5(3), 8(1), and 11(2) instead.

### Section 312(2) — annual rating by-law authority (verbatim)

> **312 (2)** For purposes of raising the general local municipality levy, a local municipality shall, each year, pass a by-law levying a separate tax rate, as specified in the by-law, on the assessment in each property class in the local municipality rateable for local municipality purposes. 2001, c. 25, s. 312 (2).

Source: [Ontario Municipal Act, 2001](https://www.ontario.ca/laws/docs/01m25_e.doc), section 312(2).

PACT's amending by-law definition of "annual rating by-law" in s. 4.3(a)(i) cites this provision.

### Strong-mayor framework (Part VI.1 of the Municipal Act + O. Reg. 530/22)

Bill 3 (Strong Mayors, Building Homes Act, 2022) added Part VI.1 to the Municipal Act effective November 23, 2022. O. Reg. 530/22 designates the municipalities to which Part VI.1 applies and specifies operational details.

**London is designated municipality #106 in Schedule 1 of O. Reg. 530/22.** Source: [O. Reg. 530/22, retrieved as Word document](https://www.ontario.ca/laws/docs/220530_e.doc), Schedule 1.

The relevant veto framework is in **section 284.11 of the Act itself**, not the regulation. Key verbatim subsections:

> **284.11 (5)** Subject to subsection (6), if the head of council is of the opinion that all or part of the by-law could potentially interfere with a prescribed provincial priority, the head of council may veto the by-law by providing to the clerk, on the day of the veto, a written veto document that includes the veto and the reasons for the veto. 2022, c. 18, Sched. 2, s. 1; 2022, c. 24, Sched. 3, s. 7.
>
> **284.11 (9)** Within 21 days, or such other prescribed time period, after the day the clerk provides the written veto document to the members of council under clause (7) (a), council may override the head of council's veto if two-thirds of the members of council vote to override the veto. 2022, c. 18, Sched. 2, s. 1.
>
> **284.11 (10)** For greater certainty, the head of council may vote as a member of council in a vote to override a veto. 2022, c. 18, Sched. 2, s. 1.

Source: [Ontario Municipal Act, 2001](https://www.ontario.ca/laws/docs/01m25_e.doc), section 284.11.

O. Reg. 530/22 s. 5(1) provides one important carve-out: the veto power **does not apply to by-laws under sections 289 and 290** of the Act (upper-tier municipality estimates and local municipality estimates, i.e., budget by-laws):

> **5. (1)** Section 284.11 of the Act does not apply with respect to by-laws under sections 289 and 290 of the Act.

Source: [O. Reg. 530/22](https://www.ontario.ca/laws/docs/220530_e.doc), section 5(1).

**The PACT amending by-law is NOT a budget by-law under s. 289 or s. 290.** It is a remuneration amending by-law enacted under the broad authority in ss. 5/8/11. Strong-mayor veto therefore applies to PACT. The two-thirds override threshold in s. 284.11(9) is the relevant exit ramp if the Mayor were to veto.

### Mayor and Councillor coverage (status: BLOCKER 5)

By-law CPOL.-70(b)-220 uses the phrase "elected officials" throughout. Its section 2.1 ("Definitions") says "Not applicable," so the Policy itself defines no terms. Coverage of the Mayor under sections 1.1, 3.1, and 4.2 therefore rests on standard Ontario municipal-law usage of "elected officials," which in normal usage means the head of council (the Mayor) and the other members of council (Councillors).

Resolution 2025-C06 clause (e) (November 4, 2025) says the Mayor's "current compensation BE MAINTAINED," which most plausibly means the existing $168,908 base figure is the starting point and continues to be subject to section 4.2's annual indexing in subsequent years, distinct from the one-time base reset that applies only to Councillors under clause (a).

This reading should be confirmed by the City Solicitor before the amendment text's "all elected officials including the Mayor" language is locked in. This is BLOCKER 5 above.

## Part 5: The PACT refactor design

### What PACT leaves untouched (subject to BLOCKERS 1-5 and 8)

The City has spent significant public process building the existing framework. The PACT refactor is designed to leave all of the following in place: the base reset under resolution 2025-C06 clauses (a) and (d), the section 4.2 annual indexing formula, the automatic-administration provision in clause (c), the Mayor's maintained base under clause (e), the agencies-boards-commissions practice (clause f), the Deputy Mayor and Budget Chair stipend mechanism contemplated under clause (h). Subject to BLOCKER 4 confirmation, the override of section 4.2(a)'s zero floor is intended to apply only in years with a levy increase. Section 4.2(b) (the non-union staff wage-freeze pause) is discussed separately below.

### Interaction with s. 4.2(b) wage-freeze pause

Section 4.2(b) of CPOL.-70(b)-220 reads: "in those years where non-union staff wages are frozen, no increase shall be applied." On a plain reading, the clause suppresses positive adjustments only. The language "no increase shall be applied" does not on its face address negative adjustments. Under this reading, in a non-union wage-freeze year that is also a levy-increase year, the section 4.2 raw adjustment is zero (per the freeze), and PACT's reduction (a negative number of percentage points) still applies, producing a net negative adjustment.

An alternative reading is that the clause is shorthand for "no adjustment of any kind shall be applied," in which case both positive and negative adjustments are paused in a freeze year. The City Solicitor should confirm which reading governs.

The drafting choice in v5's section 4.3 is the plain-reading interpretation. If the Solicitor concludes the broader reading is correct, the amendment text can be revised to explicitly address freeze years.

### What PACT adds

PACT adds one piece, and only one piece, that the existing framework does not contain: a connection between the property tax levy council adopts and the pay council pays itself. Under the existing framework, councillors who vote for a large levy increase face no direct consequence in their own compensation. PACT closes that gap by reducing the otherwise-payable section 4.2 adjustment by the same number of percentage points as the levy increase council itself approved.

### Interaction with clause (c) automatic administration

Resolution 2025-C06 clause (c) authorizes the Civic Administration (the City Clerk) to apply the annual adjustment in Councillor compensation automatically, without further Council direction each year. PACT's amendment is consistent with this principle. Once the amending by-law is enacted, the levy-link calculation in section 4.3 becomes part of the formula the City Clerk applies on January 1. The PACT amending by-law itself provides the authority for automatic application of the levy-link, by the same logic that clause (c) extended automatic administration to the section 4.2 calculation. No additional Council motion is required each year.

The Clerk's two-step calculation under PACT (compute section 4.2 raw adjustment; reduce by levy increase) is no more complex than the existing single-step section 4.2 calculation already required under clause (c). Both inputs are published municipal data points (Statistics Canada census variation and the annual rating by-law levy figure).

### Mechanism

For any fiscal year:

1. The City Clerk computes the section 4.2 raw adjustment.
2. The City Clerk determines the percentage change in the total municipal tax levy adopted by Council in the annual rating by-law for that fiscal year compared to the immediately preceding fiscal year.
3. If the levy change is positive, the raw adjustment is reduced by the same number of percentage points as the levy increase. The result is the PACT-adjusted compensation change.
4. If the levy change is zero or negative, the section 4.2 raw adjustment applies unchanged.
5. The resulting adjustment is applied on January 1 to all elected officials' remuneration, including base compensation and any stipends paid under the Policy as it may be amended from time to time.
6. The PACT-adjusted result may be negative. There is no floor.

> The 3.5% raw-adjustment figure used in these scenarios is the 2015-2020 average from the Task Force agenda. The operative rate for any year from 2027 onward will be derived from the most recent census period at the time of application. Operational figures used in any actual PACT calculation will come from (a) the relevant Statistics Canada publication for the section 4.2 raw adjustment and (b) the City's annual rating by-law for the levy increase. Treat the table below as an arithmetic illustration of the PACT mechanism, not as a forecast or commitment.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Flat levy year | 3.5% (placeholder) | 0% | 3.5% | Existing system unchanged |

## Part 6: The amending by-law text

### Proposed amending by-law

> **A by-law to amend By-law No. CPOL.-70(b)-220 (Remuneration for Elected Officials and Appointed Citizen Members Policy)**
>
> WHEREAS the Council of The Corporation of the City of London is authorized under section 5(3), section 8(1), and paragraphs 1 and 3 of subsection 11(2) of the Municipal Act, 2001, S.O. 2001, c. 25, to pass by-laws respecting the governance structure and financial management of the municipality, including the remuneration of its elected officials;
>
> AND WHEREAS By-law No. CPOL.-70(b)-220 (Remuneration for Elected Officials and Appointed Citizen Members Policy) governs the adjustment of remuneration for elected officials of the City of London;
>
> AND WHEREAS the property tax levy adopted by Council in each fiscal year reflects Council's exercise of its fiscal authority and represents the most direct decision through which Council determines the financial burden borne by City of London property taxpayers;
>
> AND WHEREAS the Council of The Corporation of the City of London considers it appropriate, in the interests of fiscal accountability, alignment between elected official remuneration and the fiscal capacity decisions taken by Council, and the continued public confidence in the integrity of municipal compensation, to align the annual adjustment of elected official remuneration with the property tax levy decisions made by Council in each fiscal year;
>
> AND WHEREAS this section does not affect the base compensation set or maintained by Council in any other resolution or by-law and does not affect Council's authority to set the property tax levy in any year;
>
> THE MUNICIPAL COUNCIL OF THE CORPORATION OF THE CITY OF LONDON ENACTS as follows:
>
> 1. By-law No. CPOL.-70(b)-220 is hereby amended by adding the following section:
>
>    **4.3 Levy-Linked Adjustment.**
>
>    a) **Definitions.** For the purpose of this section:
>      i. **"annual rating by-law"** means the by-law passed by Council in each fiscal year under subsection 312(2) of the Municipal Act, 2001 that sets the rates of taxation upon the assessment of the lands and premises in the City of London for the purpose of raising the general local municipality levy for that year;
>      ii. **"total municipal tax levy"** for a fiscal year means the total amount identified in the annual rating by-law as the amount to be levied by the City of London for municipal purposes, as that figure is originally adopted by Council in the annual rating by-law for that year. The total municipal tax levy excludes, to the extent each is separately identified in the annual rating by-law: (A) any amount levied for education purposes as set by the Province of Ontario, and (B) any amount identified as arising from changes in assessment values determined by the Municipal Property Assessment Corporation. If any such amount is not separately identified in the annual rating by-law, the total municipal tax levy is the figure as presented in the by-law for municipal purposes without further reduction. The total municipal tax levy is not adjusted for later supplementary estimates, supplementary assessments, or mid-year true-ups unless the annual rating by-law itself is formally amended. Where the total municipal tax levy for a fiscal year cannot be determined from the annual rating by-law, the determination shall be made by the City Treasurer and documented in the records of the office of the City Clerk;
>      iii. **"levy increase percentage"** for a fiscal year means the percentage change in the total municipal tax levy for that fiscal year compared to the total municipal tax levy for the immediately preceding fiscal year, calculated as ((L_t − L_{t-1}) / L_{t-1}) × 100, where L_t is the total municipal tax levy for the fiscal year in question and L_{t-1} is the total municipal tax levy for the immediately preceding fiscal year, with the result rounded to two decimal places. The resulting value is a numerical quantity to be treated as percentage points for the purpose of subsection (b). Where L_{t-1} is zero or where the total municipal tax levy for the immediately preceding fiscal year cannot be determined, the levy increase percentage for that fiscal year shall be deemed to be zero;
>      iv. **"remuneration"** as used in this section means the base compensation of an elected official as set under this Policy, and any additional stipend or allowance paid to an elected official under this Policy as it may be amended from time to time, including without limitation any stipend paid to the Deputy Mayor or Budget Chair.
>
>    b) Notwithstanding subsections 4.2(a) and 4.2(b) of this Policy, in any fiscal year in which the levy increase percentage is greater than zero, the annual adjustment to the remuneration of City of London elected officials under section 4.2 shall be reduced by a number of percentage points equal to the levy increase percentage. If the levy increase percentage is less than or equal to zero, this subsection (b) does not apply.
>
>    c) The reduction under subsection (b) applies even where the resulting annual adjustment is negative.
>
>    d) This section first applies to the annual adjustment under section 4.2 calculated for the first January 1 occurring after this by-law comes into force. The section 4.2 adjustment for January 1 of a given calendar year uses the total municipal tax levy from the annual rating by-law for the fiscal year that began before that January 1, compared to the total municipal tax levy for the immediately preceding fiscal year.
>
>    e) For greater certainty, this section does not alter the base compensation of any elected official as set or maintained by Council under any other resolution or by-law, and does not restrict or alter Council's authority to set the property tax levy in any year.
>
>    f) If there is any conflict between this section and any other provision of this Policy, this section prevails to the extent of the conflict. For greater certainty, this subsection applies only to interpretation conflicts within section 4 of this Policy relating to annual adjustment and levy-linkage.
>
>    g) All percentage and percentage-point calculations under this section shall be rounded to two decimal places at each computational step.
>
> 2. This by-law shall come into force and effect on the day of its passage.

### Drafting notes

- **Section number placeholders from v9 are now filled.** WHEREAS cites s. 5(3), s. 8(1), and s. 11(2) paragraphs 1 and 3. Annual rating by-law cites s. 312(2). Verified verbatim against the consolidated Act.
- **Policy rationale in WHEREAS clauses.** v7 expands the WHEREAS clauses to anchor PACT in fiscal accountability, alignment between remuneration and fiscal capacity decisions, and continued public confidence. This rebuts the "punitive" or "arbitrary" framing that opposition or counsel may raise.
- **"Total municipal tax levy" anchors on London's own published figure.** The definition uses the City's "municipal purposes" total in the annual rating by-law as the primary reference, with education and MPAC exclusions as secondary clarifiers. This moves the risk from hand-written exclusion categories into the City's own consistent reporting.
- **Levy figure is from the rating by-law as originally adopted.** Supplementary estimates, supplementary assessments, and mid-year true-ups do not adjust the figure used in the PACT calculation. This prevents arguments that the levy increase should reflect realized collections or post-adoption modifications.
- **"Levy increase percentage" uses explicit arithmetic.** The formula ((L_t − L_{t-1}) / L_{t-1}) × 100 removes any unit-of-measurement ambiguity. The reduction in subsection (b) treats this percentage as a number of percentage points to subtract from the section 4.2 adjustment.
- **Worked example:** if the section 4.2 raw adjustment = 3.5% and the levy increase percentage = 2.0, the PACT-adjusted change = 3.5 − 2.0 = 1.5 (percentage points), or 1.5%. If section 4.2 raw = 3.5% and levy increase = 5.0, PACT-adjusted = 3.5 − 5.0 = −1.5%, a pay reduction.
- **Zero-denominator edge case.** Subsection (a)(iii) handles the case where the prior-year levy is zero or unavailable by deeming the levy increase to be zero for that year. This prevents a divide-by-zero crash in the formula.
- **"Notwithstanding subsections 4.2(a) and 4.2(b)"** in subsection (b) explicitly overrides both the zero floor and the wage-freeze pause. This removes the interpretive argument that the override applies only to the floor.
- **Subsection (e) is a for-greater-certainty clause.** PACT does not touch Council's base-setting authority or the property tax levy decision itself. It only affects the annual adjustment computation. This makes the boundaries of PACT explicit and forecloses overreach challenges.
- **Subsection (f) is the priority/conflict clause.** If future amendments to the Policy create interpretive conflict with PACT, PACT prevails to the extent of the conflict. Standard municipal drafting technique.
- **"Remuneration" definition uses "as it may be amended from time to time"** so that future stipends (e.g., the clause (h) Deputy Mayor / Budget Chair 8% stipend) are caught when they are formalized in the Policy, without requiring PACT to be re-amended.
- **Counsel review of the section 4.2 override** (subsections (b) and (c)) remains BLOCKER 4.

## Part 7: Implementation path

### Procedural mechanics

Three procedural paths are available; recommended path is the first.

1. **Strategic Priorities and Policy Committee referral.** Standard path for amendments to CPOL-series by-laws. Same path used for the November 4, 2025 resolution itself.
2. **Direct Council motion.** Higher procedural risk.
3. **Embedded in the next Council Resourcing Review.** Slower but creates the lowest political-friction context.

### Strong-mayor veto exposure (legal, subject to BLOCKER 3)

Per s. 284.11 of the Municipal Act (verbatim text in Part 1), the amending by-law is subject to strong-mayor veto authority. The Mayor could veto if of the opinion that PACT could potentially interfere with a prescribed provincial priority. Council can override the veto with a two-thirds majority vote per s. 284.11(9).

The PACT amending by-law is NOT a budget by-law under s. 289 or s. 290 and therefore is not within the O. Reg. 530/22 s. 5(1) carve-out. Veto exposure applies.

The strong-mayor veto is triggered by the Mayor's opinion that the by-law could interfere with a prescribed provincial priority, not by the Mayor's personal financial interest. A mayor who opposes PACT on principled or political grounds can still veto it on a provincial-priority rationale regardless of whether the Mayor's own pay is affected.

### Strong-mayor symmetry (political observation only — NOT a legal argument)

**The content of this subsection is campaign-facing political commentary. It has no legal effect and should not be relied on for any legal conclusion about strong-mayor exposure.**

PACT's design applies symmetrically to the Mayor's remuneration: in years with a levy increase, the Mayor's section 4.2 adjustment is reduced on the same terms as Councillors'. As a campaign talking point this removes one possible rhetorical line of attack ("Matt only wants to cut councillor pay, not mayoral pay") and aligns the Mayor's incentives with everyone else's on the levy decision. As a legal matter it provides no protection against a strong-mayor veto.

### Effective date

The amending by-law comes into force on the day of its passage. The amendment first applies to the section 4.2 annual adjustment calculated for the first January 1 occurring after passage. There is no retroactive effect on the November 15, 2026 base reset under resolution 2025-C06. The Task Force's $94,222 baseline stands; PACT operates only on subsequent annual adjustments.

If the amending by-law is passed in 2027, it first applies to the section 4.2 adjustment for January 1, 2028, based on the 2027 annual rating by-law compared to the 2026 annual rating by-law.
