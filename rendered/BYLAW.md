# Pay Accountable to City Taxpayers (PACT) Bylaw

**Status:** draft, public consultation | Version: 1.0-draft | Last substantive review: 2026-05-22

---

> **Note to readers.** This bylaw is a single-clause amendment to existing City of London By-law No. CPOL.-70(b)-220 (Remuneration for Elected Officials and Appointed Citizen Members Policy). It adds one new section — section 4.3 — to that existing instrument. It does NOT re-enact base pay, annual indexing, the zero floor, the wage-freeze pause, the recurring base reset, or any other component already established by City Council in resolution 2025-C06 (November 4, 2025). Those provisions continue to operate untouched.

## Preamble

**WHEREAS** the Council of The Corporation of the City of London is authorized under section 5(3), section 8(1), and paragraphs 1 and 3 of subsection 11(2) of the *Municipal Act, 2001*, S.O. 2001, c. 25, to pass by-laws respecting the governance structure and financial management of the municipality, including the remuneration of its elected officials;

**AND WHEREAS** By-law No. CPOL.-70(b)-220 (Remuneration for Elected Officials and Appointed Citizen Members Policy) governs the adjustment of remuneration for elected officials of the City of London;

**AND WHEREAS** the property tax levy adopted by Council in each fiscal year reflects Council's exercise of its fiscal authority and represents the most direct decision through which Council determines the financial burden borne by City of London property taxpayers;

**AND WHEREAS** the Council of The Corporation of the City of London considers it appropriate, in the interests of fiscal accountability, alignment between elected official remuneration and the fiscal capacity decisions taken by Council, and the continued public confidence in the integrity of municipal compensation, to align the annual adjustment of elected official remuneration with the property tax levy decisions made by Council in each fiscal year;

**AND WHEREAS** this section does not affect the base compensation set or maintained by Council in any other resolution or by-law and does not affect Council's authority to set the property tax levy in any year;

**THE MUNICIPAL COUNCIL OF THE CORPORATION OF THE CITY OF LONDON ENACTS as follows:**

By-law No. CPOL.-70(b)-220 is hereby amended by adding the following section.

## Section 4.3 — Levy-Linked Adjustment

**4.3(a)(i) — "annual rating by-law" defined.**
"Annual rating by-law" means the by-law passed by Council in each fiscal year under subsection 312(2) of the *Municipal Act, 2001* that sets the rates of taxation upon the assessment of the lands and premises in the City of London for the purpose of raising the general local municipality levy for that year.

**4.3(a)(ii) — "total municipal tax levy" defined.**
"Total municipal tax levy" for a fiscal year means the total amount identified in the annual rating by-law as the amount to be levied by the City of London for municipal purposes, as that figure is originally adopted by Council in the annual rating by-law for that year. The total municipal tax levy excludes, to the extent each is separately identified in the annual rating by-law: (A) any amount levied for education purposes as set by the Province of Ontario, and (B) any amount identified as arising from changes in assessment values determined by the Municipal Property Assessment Corporation. If any such amount is not separately identified in the annual rating by-law, the total municipal tax levy is the figure as presented in the by-law for municipal purposes without further reduction. The total municipal tax levy is not adjusted for later supplementary estimates, supplementary assessments, or mid-year true-ups unless the annual rating by-law itself is formally amended. Where the total municipal tax levy for a fiscal year cannot be determined from the annual rating by-law, the determination shall be made by the City Treasurer and documented in the records of the office of the City Clerk.

**4.3(a)(iii) — "levy increase percentage" defined.**
"Levy increase percentage" for a fiscal year means the percentage change in the total municipal tax levy for that fiscal year compared to the total municipal tax levy for the immediately preceding fiscal year, calculated as ((current-year total municipal tax levy − previous-year total municipal tax levy) ÷ previous-year total municipal tax levy) × 100, with the result rounded to two decimal places. The resulting value is a numerical quantity to be treated as percentage points for the purpose of clause 4.3(b). Where the total municipal tax levy for the immediately preceding fiscal year is zero or cannot be determined, the levy increase percentage for that fiscal year shall be deemed to be zero.

**4.3(a)(iv) — "remuneration" defined.**
"Remuneration" as used in this section means the base compensation of an elected official as set under this Policy, and any additional stipend or allowance paid to an elected official under this Policy as it may be amended from time to time, including without limitation any stipend paid to the Deputy Mayor or Budget Chair.

**4.3(b) — Levy-Linked Reduction.**
Notwithstanding subsections 4.2(a) and 4.2(b) of this Policy, in any fiscal year in which the levy increase percentage is greater than zero, the annual adjustment to the remuneration of City of London elected officials under section 4.2 shall be reduced by a number of percentage points equal to the levy increase percentage. If the levy increase percentage is less than or equal to zero, this subsection (b) does not apply.

**4.3(c) — Negative Adjustment Permitted.**
The reduction under subsection 4.3(b) applies even where the resulting annual adjustment is negative.

**4.3(d) — First Year of Application.**
This section first applies to the annual adjustment under section 4.2 calculated for the first January 1 occurring after this by-law comes into force. The section 4.2 adjustment for January 1 of a given calendar year uses the total municipal tax levy from the annual rating by-law for the fiscal year that began before that January 1, compared to the total municipal tax levy for the immediately preceding fiscal year.

**4.3(e) — For Greater Certainty.**
This section does not alter the base compensation of any elected official as set or maintained by Council under any other resolution or by-law, and does not restrict or alter Council's authority to set the property tax levy in any year.

**4.3(f) — Internal Priority.**
If there is any conflict between this section and any other provision of this Policy, this section prevails to the extent of the conflict. For greater certainty, this subsection applies only to interpretation conflicts within section 4 of this Policy relating to annual adjustment and levy-linkage.

**4.3(g) — Rounding.**
All percentage and percentage-point calculations under this section shall be rounded to two decimal places at each computational step.

## Coming Into Force

This by-law shall come into force and effect on the day of its passage.
