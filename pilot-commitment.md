# Pilot Commitment

This document is **not part of the bylaw.** It is a public commitment made by the candidate who drafted the bylaw and who is bringing it forward.

## The commitment

The drafting of this bylaw was conducted in public. The full text, every parameter, every section of prose, and the record of every suggested change, accepted or rejected, are available in this repository.

This was a pilot. Matt Millar, candidate for Ward 9 in the 2026 City of London municipal election, commits to using the same public-drafting process for every substantive motion brought forward during the term of office, if elected.

Direct democracy does not replace representative democracy. It complements it. A council that drafts its motions in public is a council that has nothing to hide and has the discipline to defend every choice it makes.

## Why this is a separate document

A bylaw is a legal instrument that binds the City. A personal commitment by an individual candidate or councillor is not. Keeping the two distinct keeps the legal text clean and the political commitment honest about what it is.

## Scope

This commitment applies to substantive policy motions, not to procedural votes, in-camera matters where confidentiality is legally required, or routine administrative business. The list of motions to which it has been applied will be maintained publicly during the term of office.
