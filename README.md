# The Pay Accountable to City Taxpayers (PACT) Bylaw

A proposed by-law for the City of London, Ontario, drafted in public.

If passed, this by-law adds a single new section to the City's existing remuneration policy (By-law CPOL.-70(b)-220) that ties elected official pay to the property tax levy Council itself adopts. The mechanism is a **parallel persistent ledger** called the *accumulated decrement*, applied multiplicatively on top of whatever pay section 4.2 indexing and any future base re-pegs would otherwise produce. **Each percentage point Council raises the levy adds a percentage point of permanent pay decrement that survives every future base reset.** Levy cuts erode the decrement (floored at zero; no pay raise ever generated). There is no upper cap on the decrement. The by-law does not change any other component of the City's remuneration framework — base pay, the recurring 70th-percentile base reset adopted by Council on November 4, 2025 (resolution 2025-C06), the existing annual indexing formula by census income variation, all stay exactly as Council has already set them. The audit trail shows three Perplexity reviews on this design (v13 Deep Research, v13.1 delta, v13.2 confirmation — all 2026-05-23), the final returning UNCONDITIONAL PASS.

The bylaw text, every line of the audit trail, and the per-vote tally on the related pay-raise resolution are all in this public repository. The bylaw is the policy. The process is the point.

This is a pilot. If the candidate proposing this bylaw is elected, the same public-drafting process will be used for every substantive motion brought forward during the term. Direct democracy doesn't replace council. It makes sure your councillor knows where you stand on every issue, not just at election time.

## What this by-law is, exactly

The PACT Bylaw is **an amending by-law**, not a standalone bylaw. When introduced and passed, it would carry the next available number in the City of London's CPOL.-70 series (likely CPOL.-70(c)-XXX) and would add a single new section — section 4.3 — to existing By-law CPOL.-70(b)-220. The "CPOL" prefix denotes a Council Policy category by-law; CPOL by-laws are legally still by-laws under the Municipal Act, 2001, even though the City refers to them informally as "policies."

The authority for the amending by-law is the broad permissive grant in Municipal Act sections 5(3), 8(1), 8(2), and 10(2) paragraphs 1 (governance structure), 2 (accountability and transparency) and 3 (financial management). London is a single-tier municipality, so section 10(2) rather than section 11(2) is the operative grant. Sections 312(1) and 290 supply the statutory definition of the levy the by-law measures, section 285 the fiscal year, and section 284 the annual remuneration reporting that carries the published numbers. The full statutory text and the design rationale are in [`docs/v13-proposal.md`](docs/v13-proposal.md). The architectural change from v12 to v13 (parallel persistent ledger applied multiplicatively, rather than section 4.2 modifier) is explained in detail there.
