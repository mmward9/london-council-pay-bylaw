# The Pay Accountable to City Taxpayers (PACT) Bylaw

A proposed by-law for the City of London, Ontario, drafted in public.

If passed, this by-law adds a single new section to the City's existing remuneration policy (By-law CPOL.-70(b)-220) that links the annual indexing of elected official pay to the property tax levy Council itself adopts. **If Council raises the levy, the otherwise-payable annual adjustment to elected official remuneration is reduced by the same number of percentage points.** Section 4.2's existing zero floor and wage-freeze pause are expressly overridden in years with a levy increase, so the adjustment can go negative. The by-law does not change any other component of the City's remuneration framework — base pay, the recurring 70th-percentile base reset adopted by Council on November 4, 2025, the existing annual indexing formula by census income variation, all stay exactly as Council has already set them.

The bylaw text, every line of the audit trail, the per-vote tally on the related pay-raise resolution, and the City Solicitor / Treasurer handoff memo are all in this public repository. The bylaw is the policy. The process is the point.

This is a pilot. If the candidate proposing this bylaw is elected, the same public-drafting process will be used for every substantive motion brought forward during the term. Direct democracy doesn't replace council. It makes sure your councillor knows where you stand on every issue, not just at election time.

## What this by-law is, exactly

The PACT Bylaw is **an amending by-law**, not a standalone bylaw. When introduced and passed, it would carry the next available number in the City of London's CPOL.-70 series (likely CPOL.-70(c)-XXX) and would add a single new section — section 4.3 — to existing By-law CPOL.-70(b)-220. The "CPOL" prefix denotes a Council Policy category by-law; CPOL by-laws are legally still by-laws under the Municipal Act, 2001, even though the City refers to them informally as "policies."

The authority for the amending by-law is the broad permissive grant in Municipal Act sections 5(3), 8(1), 8(2), and 11(2) paragraphs 1 (governance structure) and 3 (financial management). The full statutory text and the design rationale are in [`docs/v12-proposal.md`](docs/v12-proposal.md).
